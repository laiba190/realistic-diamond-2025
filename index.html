Understanding the Materials: Moissanite vs. Cubic Zirconia

The two primary materials behind realistic diamond simulants are moissanite and cubic zirconia (CZ). Both provide excellent sparkle but differ in durability, color, and brilliance.

Property Moissanite Cubic Zirconia
Hardness (Mohs) 9.25 — highly durable 8.5 — suitable for occasional wear
Brilliance Index 2.65 — even more sparkly than diamond 2.17 — very bright with white fire
Cost Range Moderate Low
Visual Realism Extremely realistic Highly convincing when well cut

How to Identify a Realistic Simulant

When shopping, visual cues often distinguish high-quality simulants from cheap fakes. Here are key indicators:

  1. Brilliance and Fire: A realistic simulant will refract light similarly to a diamond, without appearing glassy or overly rainbow-like.
  2. Cut Quality: Look for symmetrical facets and sharp polish lines — attributes Luxuria prioritizes.
  3. Color Grade: The most convincing stones range D–F (colorless).
  4. Setting Quality: Precious metal settings greatly enhance realism — sterling silver or gold finishes are preferred.

Care and Maintenance of Simulant Rings

Maintenance can extend the brilliance and life of simulant jewellery. Here are some professional care guidelines:

  • Clean your ring every 2–3 weeks with mild soap, warm water, and a soft toothbrush.
  • Avoid ultrasonic cleaners unless specified safe for your material.
  • Remove rings before using lotions, perfumes, or cleaning agents.
  • Store individually in a soft pouch to prevent scratching.

Luxuria recommends occasional re-polishing and rhodium re-plating for sterling silver bases to preserve the reflective finish — practices that ensure long-term shine and authenticity.
